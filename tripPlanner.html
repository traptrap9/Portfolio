Trip Planner

Collaborative Development of an Integrated Commute Planner

A Team Experience

Together with four other team members, we embarked on a collaborative project to create a comprehensive map routing web application designed to revolutionise the way users plan their commutes. Our diverse backgrounds and skillsets were harnessed to develop this Java-based application, focusing on creating a personalised and efficient commute planning system for its users.

The intent behind our product was not merely to devise a simple route planner, but to provide an integrated commuting solution that accommodates the specific requirements and preferences of individual users. Our goal was to make commuting a more seamless, intuitive, and stress-free experience.

Our application stands out in its ability to help users pinpoint convenient amenities such as bike racks and parking spaces, thus allowing them to optimise their commute paths based on their preferences. For example, a user could plan to drive a portion of their journey, park their vehicle at a specific location, and then bike the remaining distance.

Beyond the daily commute, we designed the application to provide users with valuable insights when considering potential future relocations. By visualising the impact of a new location on their commuting patterns, our users are equipped to make informed, strategic decisions. Our application, thus, recognises the complexities of modern commuting and offers personalised solutions that fit the unique needs of each user.

Adopting Agile Practises

User Stories, Story Points, MoSCoW, and Documentation

Formulating and Prioritising User Stories

By implementing user stories, a technique inherent to Agile methodologies, we represented use cases from the viewpoint of the end-user. This method ensured that our focus always remained on the needs of the user. To gauge the effort required for each user story, we employed story points, allowing us to quantify the complexity of tasks and prioritise them effectively. Furthermore, the MoSCoW method helped us categorise these user stories based on their importance and urgency, streamlining our decision-making process when deciding which features to include in each sprint.

Documentation and Team Collaboration

To maintain clear communication and alignment within the team, we held weekly meetings where we assessed our progress, addressed challenges, and planned for upcoming tasks. The outcomes and key decisions from these meetings were meticulously documented and stored in our project's GitHub wiki. The practice of thorough documentation was followed throughout the project, where every step from initial planning to final implementation was recorded in detail, serving as a reference guide and a blueprint for future enhancements. Utilising GitHub's wiki feature allowed us to maintain a single, organised, and accessible source of truth for all our project information.

These practices played a crucial role in facilitating effective project management, enhancing collaboration, and ensuring a user-centric focus in our development process.

Backend Architecture

Leveraging Spring Boot, Thymeleaf, and OpenTripPlanner in our Map Routing Application

Spring Boot, our chosen framework, offered powerful functionality and simplified the setup of our Spring application. Its embedded server eased deployment, and auto-configuration reduced manual setup work. Moreover, dependency management facilitated integration with other tools, like Thymeleaf and OTP.

Thymeleaf: Enhancing Development and Debugging

Thymeleaf, our server-side Java template engine, allowed for natural templating and direct viewing of templates in browsers, improving our development and debugging processes. Its seamless integration with Spring enabled us to use the Spring Expression Language (SpEL) for dynamic content in our HTML templates.

OpenTripPlanner: Powering Multi-modal Trip Planning

OpenTripPlanner (OTP), an open-source software, provided extensive functionality for multi-modal trip planning, vital to our application's main feature - personalised commute routes. Its powerful API allowed us to handle complex geographical and transportation data, resulting in optimal route recommendations tailored to user needs.

Combining Technologies for a Robust Backend API

By integrating these technologies, we created a robust backend API supporting our application's complex functionality and ensuring a streamlined development process. The combination of Spring Boot, Thymeleaf, and OTP helped us maintain good programming practices and offered customisation to meet our project's specific needs.
